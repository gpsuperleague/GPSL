Transfer market rules
  • Minimum bid = market value for all listed contracted players. Your first bid cannot be below the player’s market value (see the Value column).
  • If there is already a highest bid, you must bid at least ₿500,000 more than that amount (and still at least market value).
  • Direct offers to a contracted player (GPDB → Make Offer) must also be at least market value; the seller reviews the offer in Transfer Centre.
  • Reserve met when the timer ends → the highest bidder wins automatically: player moves, buyer pays, seller receives the fee.
  • Reserve not met but there are bids → the seller has 24 hours in Transfer Centre to accept or reject the best offer (listed under “Seller review” here if you tick that filter).
  • No bids when time runs out → the listing closes; the player stays with the seller.
  • Extra time (anti-sniping): if someone bids in the final 2 hours before the listed end time, the auction is extended by 1 hour (once per listing). After that, any bid in the final 5 minutes adds 5 more minutes — and that 5-minute extension can happen again if another late bid comes in.
  • Countdowns on each row are in your local time. Settlement runs shortly after the clock hits zero (usually within a few minutes).

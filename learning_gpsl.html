Getting started & club auction

Before you have a club, the site is limited to registration and the club auction. Admin registers your login; you choose an owner tag (your public name on rankings and auction leader boards) and bid on a vacant club from your starting budget.

Club auction (registration) Club auction room Login

Rules

  • Owner tag — set on registration before bidding. It is shown as the auction leader and on owner rankings. It cannot be changed once saved for the club auction.
  • Starting budget — you bid from a league-set balance (currently ₿550m for new owners). Winning bid is deducted when the club is assigned; the remainder becomes your club balance.
  • Opening bids — based on club prestige and stadium capacity (stadium cost = capacity × ₿1,000). Check capacity, expected Season 1 position, gate income, and maintenance on the auction page before you bid.
  • Countdown — club auction uses the same timed window as draft auctions (Day 1 19:00 UK start). The timer only appears on club auction pages when the club auction is enabled — not on player or manager draft pages.
  • After you win, your club is linked to your account, finances are set, and the full site opens.

Read this guide while you wait. Once you have a club, your badge appears top-left in the nav and links to Club Details.

My Club pages

  • Club Details — badge, division, nation, government subsidy cards (HG / Youth / B&B), match availability (weekly calendar), holiday booking, email/password, and club overview.
  • Squad — registered players, home-grown and under-21 counts, contracts, list/sell actions.
  • Finances — current balance and links to ledger, income, costs, and season accounts.
  • Stadium — capacity, attendance estimate, upcoming home gates, expansion.
  • Match Day — submit scores, player stats, and your match squad (after kick-off is agreed and both owners have checked in).
  • Transfer Centre — your listings, incoming offers, scouting targets, and direct deals.
  • Club History — past seasons, positions, archived results, and a trophy cabinet (click a trophy for that season’s final table or cup bracket).
  • Fixtures — your games highlighted; Propose time / Respond opens the scheduling page for that match.

Finances

Overview Ledger Posted income Posted costs Season accounts

How money moves

  • Balance — your spendable club cash. Transfers, wages, fines, and prizes all post here via the ledger.
  • Gate receipts — league home matches: 100% to the home club. Formula: capacity × fill rate × ₿20/seat. Fill depends on table position and recent history. Cup ties split 50/50.
  • Stadium maintenance — seasonal cost based on capacity (12.5% × capacity × ₿1,500). Shown on Stadium and in season accounts.
  • Government subsidies — HG, Youth, and B&B targets on Club Details; payments appear in season accounts when earned.
  • Season accounts — workbook-style view: Posted (ledger total this season), Breakdown by type, Running total, and Pending (forecast not yet on the ledger). Projected balance = current + pending.

Fines, cup prizes, challenge payouts, and transfer fees all arrive as inbox messages and ledger lines.

Big & medium club expectations

Clubs are ranked by prestige (top 10 = big, 11–35 = medium, rest = low). Each season the league compares your expected performance (from prestige rank and, for medium/low clubs, manager rating) to your actual league finish and cup results. See Stadium for fill targets and performance bands (on target / slight / bad / abysmal).

What “expectation” means

  • Expected position — derived from prestige rank (better clubs are expected to finish higher).
  • Medium & low clubs — a strong manager rating can raise the expectation (you are expected to finish closer to the top).
  • Big clubs — manager rating does not lower the bar; big clubs are always held to a high standard.
  • Missing expectation also affects stadium gate fill and can trigger attendance penalties — not only player unrest.

Player transfer request (if you miss the target)

Checked at season archive (end of season). At most one forced listing per club per season.

  • Big clubs — one random player from your top four rated squad members requests a transfer. Any age. Listed at market value on the Transfer Market.
  • Medium clubs — same rule, but only players rated 74–78 who are over 21 (22 or older) can be chosen.
  • Low clubs — no underperformance transfer requests.

Forced listing rules

  • Reserve = market value — buyers bid at least MV; normal transfer window rules apply to purchasers.
  • Perpetual relisting — if the listing window closes with no sale, it reopens automatically at the current market value.
  • Cannot remove — you cannot cancel or “Remove” the listing from Transfer Centre.
  • Below reserve — if a bid is below MV you may still accept; you cannot reject a forced listing back off the market (it relists instead).
  • Normal same-season and final-year listing blocks are waived for these forced requests — the player is pushing to leave.
  • You get an Inbox message naming the player and linking to Transfer Centre.

This is separate from manager retention (manager released if league target missed) — you can face both in the same season. See Managers below.

Squad & contracts

Rules are enforced when you sign players — warnings appear before you bid or confirm.

Squad size & composition

  • Maximum 28 registered players. You can still bid at 28, but a 29th signing triggers an automatic release of your highest-rated player who was not signed this season.
  • If a foreign sale slot is available, that route is used (no fine). Otherwise the player is released for market value, your club is fined ₿10,000,000, and the player cannot be signed until next season.
  • Home-grown — minimum 8 players whose nation matches your club nation.
  • Under-21 — minimum 5 players aged 21 or younger.

Contracts (summary)

  • Standard contracts run in 3-season cycles. Track expiry on Expiring Contracts and the squad page.
  • Final season — you cannot list or sell the player on the transfer market. They enter the hidden wage auction at expiry (all clubs, including yours, may bid once) — unless they are a legacy card (see below).
  • Home-grown ≤23 — protected from the expiry auction; uncontested renewal applies while still eligible. Protection ends at age 24.
  • Winning an expiry bid gives a new 3-season contract at the bid wage from season rollover.

Legacy cards (off PESDB)

When admin syncs GPDB with pesdb.net, players still in GPSL but no longer on the site are marked legacy. They stay at their club and remain playable.

  • Cannot be sold or listed on the transfer market at any time.
  • Not on the expiring-contracts wage bid market — other clubs cannot bid for them.
  • In the final contract year, renew from Squad: one season at a time (not a new 3-year deal). Home-grown ≤23 may keep the same wage on renew.
  • Or choose Expire — release for MV from Squad to drop the player for market value.
  • If the card returns on a future sync, it becomes a normal GPDB card again.

Managers

Manager Database Manager Market Manager Draft Auction
  • Each club has one manager. Their rating and your division set a league finish target for the season.
  • Miss the target and the manager may be released at season end (see inbox / season rollover messages).
  • Missing expectations can also trigger a player transfer request on big and medium clubs — see Big & medium club expectations.
  • You may sack a manager from squad/club pages when rules allow; vacancies block new manager draft bids until filled.
  • Manager draft auctions use the same timed window as player drafts but are controlled by a separate enable flag — only active when admin turns on the manager draft.
  • When the random finish passes, winning bids are settled automatically (manager assigned, fee debited). If a club already has a manager, that auction closes without a transfer.
  • Season expectations message arrives in your Inbox when a manager is signed.

Transfers & markets

Transfer window

  • When the transfer window is open, list players from the squad or Transfer Centre, bid on Transfer Market listings, and accept or reject direct offers.
  • Completed deals post to finances and appear in your Inbox.
  • Scouting targets — save players from GPDB; review them under Transfer Centre → Scouting Targets.
  • Legacy cards — players removed from pesdb.net stay at their club but cannot be sold; see Squad & contracts and Legacy players.

Selling abroad

  • Limited foreign club sale slots per season — using one avoids the squad overflow fine when you need to free a place.
  • Check squad warnings before confirming any signing or winning bid.

Underperformance listings

  • If a big or medium club misses its season expectation, a player may be forced onto the market at MV with perpetual relisting — you cannot delist them.
  • Full rules: Big & medium club expectations.

Auctions & drafts

Important: Club auction, player draft, and manager draft are independent. Admin enables each one separately. A countdown on a page only appears when that auction type is active — enabling club auction does not turn on player or manager draft timers elsewhere.

Club auction (pre-launch)

Registration Auction room
  • Vacant clubs only. Highest bidder wins when the window closes and admin/engine settles.
  • Winning bid is charged; remaining budget becomes your club balance.

Player draft auction

GPDB Draft auction list
  • Timed window: opens Day 1 19:00 UK, bids until Day 2 18:00, then a secret random finish between 18:50 and 18:59 — the countdown never shows the exact finish time in advance.
  • Draft credits limit how many players you can win. Favourite listings on GPDB for quick access.
  • Highest bid wins each player when the draft settles.

Manager draft auction

MGDB Manager draft list
  • Same schedule pattern as player draft when enabled (Day 1 19:00 UK start, secret random finish on Day 2).
  • You may only lead one manager auction at a time — finish or get outbid before bidding elsewhere.
  • Open a free agent from MGDB to start their draft thread, or bid from the Manager draft list.
  • After the random finish, winners are assigned automatically within about a minute — check Club Details for your new manager. The draft list clears once settlement runs.

Special auctions

Special Auction
  • Occasional league events (e.g. lowest unique bid, snap auction) for cash or player prizes. Appears in the nav when live.

Match scheduling

League fixtures are published when the season starts. You and your opponent must agree a kick-off before playing. Set weekly availability on Club Details, then use Fixtures → Schedule (or Inbox) to propose or respond.

Club Details (availability) Fixtures Schedule page Inbox

GPSL month (Fri 19:00 UK → Fri 19:00 UK)

  • One real-world week = one GPSL month (August–May). The nav badge shows the active month.
  • Arrange — you can agree a time from when fixtures exist (including pre-season). For an September fixture, the primary deadline is to have it arranged before August closes.
  • Play — the match should be played in its fixture GPSL month. If it passes still unplayed, it becomes a catch-up fixture (highlighted on Fixtures) and can be played in a later month.

1 — Availability

  • Club Details → Edit weekly availability — 30-minute blocks (UK time) Mon–Sun when you are usually free.
  • Set your timezone so kick-off times show in your local clock on the schedule page.
  • Holidays (up to 14 days/season) block availability and can unlock early result entry — book only before that GPSL month opens.

2 — Agree a time

  • Home club proposes first — pick a mutual slot (both calendars intersect in the proposal window).
  • Away owner gets an Inbox message — Accept or counter-propose another mutual slot.
  • After two proposals each, the site suggests agreeing on Discord, then picking a slot on the schedule page.
  • Response deadlines apply while negotiating — the site tracks overdue turns during the month. Missed response fines (default ₿2.5m) are assessed when that fixture’s play GPSL month locks, not instantly mid-week.

3 — Check in & play

  • At the agreed kick-off, both owners open the schedule page and Check in within 10 minutes.
  • When both have checked in, Enter result on Match Day unlocks for that 30-minute block.
  • Both miss check-in — no fine. Use Pick new time to play this month on the schedule page (does not use your reschedule allowance), agree a new slot, and check in at the new kick-off.
  • One checks in, one misses — the site records a no-show but does not forfeit instantly. If you still play and confirm a normal result, no no-show fine. If the play month ends still unplayed, the no-show club gets 3–0 forfeit + ₿5m fine at month lock.
  • Voluntary drop (≥24h before kick-off) — uses your one reschedule per GPSL month; returns to scheduling with no forfeit.
  • Emergency drop (<24h before kick-off) — uses one of two per season; if you have none left, you forfeit 3–0 and a fine.
  • Mutual override — either club can request play now or a new time before the agreed kick-off; both must confirm in Inbox.

4 — Catch-up

  • If a league fixture’s play month closes still unplayed, it is flagged catch-up on Fixtures.
  • Propose new times in the current GPSL month window; stale agreed kick-offs can be reset without using the monthly reschedule allowance.

Scheduling fines (league — assessed at month lock)

  • Match Management (₿10m) — home never proposed by the arrangement deadline; can repeat each month until a proposal is made.
  • Late arrangement (₿5m) — first home proposal in the last 24h before the play month opens (either/or with Match Management at that lock).
  • Missed response (₿2.5m) — still negotiating with an overdue response when the play month locks.
  • No-show at agreed kick-off (₿5m + 3–0) — one club checked in, the other did not, and the match never received a normal result before month lock.

Fines post to your balance and appear in Inbox. Admins can apply compensation for site errors — contact league admin on Discord if something looks wrong.

Inbox messages

  • Match time proposed / countered — Accept from Inbox or open Schedule to counter.
  • Match time agreed — check in at kick-off.
  • Reschedule / replay / catch-up reset — opponent reopened scheduling; home proposes again.
  • Fine applied / compensation — scheduling or matchday tariff; see note for fixture and reason.

Match day & results

Match Day Fixtures
  • Each real-world week (Fri 19:00 UK → Fri 19:00 UK) is one GPSL month — see the calendar badge in the nav.
  • You need an agreed kick-off and both check-ins before you can enter a result (see Match scheduling). If you missed check-in, use Pick new time to play this month on the schedule page first.
  • Catch-up fixtures (play month passed, still unplayed) can be submitted in a later active GPSL month once re-scheduled.
  • Submit scores and player stats on Match Day. Set your match squad (11 starters + subs) on the pitch tab; starters auto-tick in the stats table.
  • Your opponent confirms or rejects — you are notified at each step via the inbox.
  • League: win 3 pts, draw 1, loss 0. Cup ties cannot end in a draw — play extra time / penalties if needed.
  • Confirmed results post gate receipts (where applicable) and update tables, stats, and challenges.
  • If you booked a holiday overlapping the GPSL month, you may enter results early before the normal Friday unlock — see Club Details.

League & cups

60 clubs: SuperLeague (20) plus two Championship divisions (20 each). Promotion, relegation, and playoffs follow the pyramid rules on the tables page.

Cups

  • League Cup — 60-club knockout. Prestige cups (Super8, Plate, Shield, Bowl) qualify from league standings.
  • Cup round fees and prizes post to both clubs when matches are confirmed.
  • World Cup — national teams; owner nations draft in ranking order (see International).

Nation & World Cup

Nation selection National team World Cup
  • When the selection window opens, owners pick a national team in owner ranking order (rolling four-season points).
  • You receive an inbox message when it is your turn — use Nation selection to claim a nation.
  • Manage squads and fixtures on National team. World Cup cycles add extra ranking points for owners.
  • Track standing on Owner rankings (rolling, season-by-season, and all-time tabs).

Rankings & challenges

Owner rankings Season challenges

Owner rankings

  • Points from league position and cup runs each season. Rolling four seasons sets World Cup nation draft order.
  • The Owner column shows your owner tag, not the club short name.

Season challenges

  • Admin-set targets for the current season (e.g. goals, wins, clean sheets). Prizes post instantly when a match is confirmed.
  • First club to complete all targets in a window earns the period bonus.

Season rhythm & inbox

Inbox Dashboard
  • At month start you receive a match preview (opponents, form, threats).
  • Transfer news, fines, cup draws, nation picks, match scheduling, and finance postings all go to the inbox — most messages link straight to the relevant page.
  • End of season: awards (Golden Boot, Ballon d'Or, etc.), manager retention, archived history, and season overview messages.
  • Unread count appears on the Inbox nav link.

Club expectations (summary)

  • Strong stadium attendance — underperformance reduces gate income and can trigger penalties (Stadium).
  • Big and medium clubs that miss the season target may lose a star player via a forced transfer listing — full rules.
  • Stay within squad limits and composition minimums (Squad).
  • Meet manager targets or plan for a change at season end.
  • Respond to inbox actions promptly (confirm results, accept deals, pick your nation when called, accept or counter match times).

Recent updates (2026)

Summary of newer features and rule changes — also woven into the sections above.

GPDB & player values

Admin can sync the Player Database from pesdb.net between seasons (Season Break tools). After a sync, matched players get updated stats; new pesdb cards appear as free agents; missing cards become legacy.

  • Rating in GPDB and squad may show as 85 (95) — current OVR plus calculated potential used for economics.
  • Pot. column — calculated potential (league formula), not always the same as the raw pesdb max level.
  • Market value and maximum reserve price follow the league spreadsheet formulas (rating, potential, age, position). Maximum reserve is 1.5× market value.
  • Stored values update when admin applies a sync — day-to-day screens use the database figures on listings, squad, and transfers.

Legacy players

Cards that leave pesdb.net but remain in GPSL are tagged legacy. They still count for squad, home-grown, and match day. Transfer rules are restricted — full detail on Legacy players and under Squad & contracts.

  • Not sellable and not on the expiry wage auction.
  • Final-year renewals: 1 season at a time from Squad.
  • League-wide list: Transfers → Legacy players.

Match scheduling (July 2026)

Full rules: Match scheduling. Summary — arrange from pre-season; play in the fixture month or catch up later; check in at kick-off; fines at GPSL month lock (arrangement, missed response, no-show if still unplayed). Manager draft auto-settlement

  • After the Day 2 random finish, winning manager bids settle automatically (fee debited, manager assigned to club).
  • If the high bidder already has a manager, that listing closes without a transfer.
  • The Manager draft list should clear within about a minute of finish — refresh Club Details to see your signing.

Player career honours

  • Player career pages show winner medals (league and cup) for players who met appearance thresholds.
  • League medals need 5+ league apps that season; cup medals need 1+ cup appearance.

Championship Team of the Month

  • Separate TOTM panels for Championship A and Championship B on Stats, in addition to Super League TOTM.

Club Details
